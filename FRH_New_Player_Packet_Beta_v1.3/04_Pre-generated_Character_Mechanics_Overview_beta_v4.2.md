# Sample Characters You Could Play

## Investigative Roles

Each investigator brings a different set of strengths to the group. Some specialize in research and analysis, others in social interaction, field investigation, combat, or unusual supernatural abilities.

Successful investigations often involve cooperation between several investigators using different approaches to uncover the truth.

These characters are not templates. They are examples of how a `375`-point character exists within the structure of the Followers of the Right Hand.

Each demonstrates:

- Secret power in a non-magical world
- Mandatory `FRH Patron / Duty / Enemy` structure
- A different philosophy toward disadvantages
- A different risk profile

You are not required to play one of these. But they show what this campaign looks like when built correctly.

## Standard Packet Pregens

### Amber Deliamber

Archetype: `The Quiet Architect`

Amber demonstrates the subtle support-investigator lane. She combines administrative competence, occult perception, and ethical restraint. She is powerful without being loud.

What she highlights:

- subtle psychic capability in a non-magical world
- structural and ethical disadvantages rather than explosive instability
- strong support for research, reading people, and controlled scene framing

### Crispin Pollick

Archetype: `The Intellectual Extremist`

Crispin is the high-risk researcher. He represents the investigator who can understand too much and push too far.

What he highlights:

- knowledge-adjacent power
- curiosity as a structural danger, not a decorative quirk
- a mechanically competent but psychologically fragile occult scholar

### Matthew Bridges

Archetype: `The Grounded Operative`

Matthew is the practical field investigator with extraordinary psychometric reach. He combines wealth, mobility, intelligence, and investigative utility.

What he highlights:

- strong field competence
- hidden power with broad clue leverage
- how wealth and competence can matter without replacing mystery

### Chaney Markov

Archetype: `The Disciplined Weapon`

Chaney represents the physically terrifying but controlled investigator. He is not flashy; he is force under discipline.

What he highlights:

- concealed resilience and strength
- heavy structural obligations and secrets
- how combat power can exist inside FRH rather than outside it

### Melody Manners

Archetype: `The Social Occultist`

Melody is the socially adept occultist whose power is easy to underestimate. She brings performance, presence, and dangerous subtlety.

What she highlights:

- social dominance over brute force

- how a player-facing occultist can feel period-appropriate rather than generic fantasy

### Mila Johannson

Archetype: `The Ancient Hunter`

Mila is a deliberate exception-case veteran. She demonstrates how age, history, and entanglement can justify a heavier burden of enemies, secrets, and supernatural recognition.

What she highlights:

- historical weight as liability

## Structural Notes

Every FRH investigator is built inside a common framework:

- `Patron (Followers of the Right Hand)`
- `Duty (Involuntary, Extremely Hazardous)`
- `Enemy (Unknown Enemies of the FRH)`
- at least two meaningful secrets

The differences between characters should come from:

- what kind of hidden power they carry
- what kinds of disadvantages shape them
- what sort of clue vectors they create
- what kind of cost they impose on themselves and the campaign
