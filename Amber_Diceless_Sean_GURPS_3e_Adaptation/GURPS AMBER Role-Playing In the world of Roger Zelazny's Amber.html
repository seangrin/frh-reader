Role-Playing In the world of Roger Zelazny's Amber


Adapted by Sean Grinslade from the Amber Dice-less Role-Playing Game by Eric Wujcik



GURPS AMBER ROLE-PLAYING


Amber characters differ from other GURPS characters in important ways. First
they are really powerful and hence expensive. Second, beginning characters
will mere novices compared to the elder Amberites and Chaos Lords. Third,
players will have a litle less control over their characters backgrounds than in
normal GURPS adventures. In Amber, players can choose what attributes,
powers, and special items they have at the start. They will not be able to
choose their parents or their friends. The characters may not even know their
parents' or friends' identities.


Another difference is how skills are utilized. Since Amber is a game of mood
and perception, having a skill listed on a character sheet does not confer
complete understanding. In regular GURPS adventures it is suffice to say. "I
have an electrical engineering skill of 16, so I go in the shop and build a
modem." Since much of the Amber universe and the skills that influence it are
very subjective, using most skills will be more player intense. For example,
Wesley has a high Shadow Manipulation skill and wants to create a pocket
shadow. He knows it can be done but has never done it himself. So instead of,
"Wesley uses his Shadow Manipulation skill of 18 to create a pocket shadow,"
the player and game master might interact like:


Player. "Wesley is going to create a pocket shadow."
GM: "How? What are you doing?"
Player: "What do you mean?"
GM: "Is Wesley just standing there thinking? Is he moving? Does he raise the
Pattern?"
Player: "Oh, yea, Wesley raises the sign of the Pattern before him and..."
GM: "Okay, that takes a while and, sure enough, the Pattern hovers before you."
Player: "Now, I create a pocket shadow."
GM: "How? You are currently just viewing the shadow your in through the Pattern."
Player. "What do I see?"
GM: "You note that you seem to be the only real object within the immediate vicinity."
Player. "What about in the whole shadow?"
GM: “It’ll take about 20 minutes to scan the whole shadow…”

Player: "Forget it, I’ll place hands in the Pattern and start pulling at the shadow stuff.”
GM: "Kinda like a tearing motion?"
Player: "Yea. Like this." [the player gestures as if tearing a piece of paper in two.]
GM: "You feel the shadow stuff give way. The lighting seems to change. It's getting darker."
Player: "What do you mean? Is the sun setting?"
GM: "No. It's more like the world is growing dimmer around you. The tear is getting bigger."
Player, "Am I still in control of the tear? Can I see anything in it?"
GM: "You feel like you have the tear under control. Through the tear it appears dark with flashes of light, like stars."
Etc.


Now the character might be on the right track to creating a pocket shadow.
Then again he could be destroying the shadow and drawing some attention
from a concerned Chaos Lord or Elder Amberite. Once the character has
learned to do something, it may not be as involved as this example. However,
things are not the same everywhere in the universe of Amber and what you
know how to do near Amber may not work near Chaos. Also, it is possible another force is acting against you. Maybe the Logrus was just waiting for a rend in that shadow at that time and will be pumping power through the hole making it larger than what Wesley wants.

When designing an Amber character it is important not to forget that most
characters will be aware of their heritage and will have been taught courtly skills.
In addition to skills like Fencing, Savoir-Faire, Heraldry(Amber or Courts), and
Riding(Horse or Flimsy), don't forget Poetry, History(Amber or Courts), Music,
Singing, Bard, Performance, Acting, Dancing, Navigation, Survival (Woodland
or Chaos), Seamanship, Tactics, Leadership, Strategy, First-Aid, Area-
Knowledge (Castle Amber, Kolvir, Courts, Shadow Earth), Bow, Staff, Knife,
and Shield. Obvious skills need to survive include Stealth, Carousing,
Gambling, Holdout, Camouflage, Driving (Car), and Poison. It is possible that
an unknown bastard grew up without influence of his/her Amber parent and just
recently came into his/her heritage. Such a character would be at disadvantage
in any courtly situation but might make up for it with a set of very well developed
skills.

A beginning Amber character is 1571 points. This may seem like a lot but the
base-line character package cost 1071. This gives the player 500 points for
powers, artifacts, creatures, and skills. Amber characters can also take up to
200 points in disadvantages (if an Elder Amberite is your enemy, it's worth major
points). The next sections define how and where you may spend your points.


BASIC AMBER PACKAGE


Amberites are tough people. This is the starting template for all Amber
characters. It is possible to lower the value on the attributes or to do away with
some native advantages to get more points for Powers or skills. This is not
recommended. Remember this is the baseline. Elder Amberites and Chaos
Lords have attributes and advantages far beyond these levels.

POWERS
The Pattern (200 points)
The Pattern is the source of power for Royal Family of Amber. It is Order. It
is a Pole of Existence. Without the Pattern this multiverse would not exist.
It is all that stands against utter Chaos. No wonder it's a pain to walk it.
In game terms, the Pattern Imprint cost 200 points and is a prerequisite for
using any of the Pattern skills. To walk the Pattern (or have walked it)
requires a HT of 22 or greater and a Will (IQ+Strong Will-Weak Will) of 18 or
greater. Beyond these requirements it will only allow those of the blood of
Oberon to walk it. If you seek an except to these rules, take it up with your
gamemaster.

Shapeshifting (80 points)
You have the ability to change your shape. This sounds really cool but it has a
couple of caveats. One, it requires tremendous energy to shape shift: 3 points
of fatigue for minor things, 10 for major changes in the same form, and 15
points to shift forms completely. Two, some forms requires changes to brain
tissues which can cause personality changes. These personality changes can
actually lead to the character being trapped in one form obeying some
instinctual

agenda and not their own. Three, because shifting is a chaotic
manifestation, it is possible to be come infected with Primal Chaos. This chaos
cancer can cripple even kill. There are doctors in the Courts who treat these
infections but the rumor is that their methods are less that gentle.
As a shapeshifter you have three natural forms that you can assume (for 15
points of fatigue) very quickly based on your skill. With practice other forms can
become well-known enough to allow changes in a 2-3 minute time frame. When
attempting to assume a new form, it can take up to hours to get it right. A
general, limited likeness (same general shape, anyway) can be assumed in a
10-20 minute time frame.

Shape Natural Forms (M/A)-this is the skill to assume one of your three
natural forms. The fatigue cost is the same, 15 points, as assuming another
form but the time is equal to 15 - skill in minutes or, if your skill is over 15, you
can change in seconds, 40 - skill in seconds. The forms are:
Human Form: This is the simplest form with no great advantages or
disadvantages. For Chaos Lords, it provides the best resistance to the
influence of the Pattern.
Demon Form: This Chaos manifestation is called demon form because
the appearance is similar to the real demons of Chaos. In this form the
character can have up to 100 points in advantages added as long as
they are bound by increased DR, natural weaponry (claws, fangs
horns), or extra limbs (wings). No skills can be increased or attributes
raised. Itis functionally the same as getting dressed in armor and
strapping on weapons. Even Amberite shapeshifters have this form,
they may just not be aware of it. Because it is a Chaos manifestation
appears in the form near the realms of Order would draw certain
attention.
Avatar Form: For Amberites, this form is usually some special animal
form. In this form, Amberites gain the natural advantages of the animal
(movement, fangs, etc.) and maintain their own attributes. For example
if Gerard was a shapeshifter he would be a mighty bear with claws and
his strength. For Chaosites, it is an elemental form based on air, fire,
water, or stone. This form is ideally suited for surviving in the chaotic
shadows near the Courts. The form is immune to whatever elemental
environment from which it comes but it cannot be assumed or
maintained in the realms of Order
Shapeshift Animal Form (M/A) - this is the ability to assume the form of some
kind of animal. In order to shift, the character observe closely the animal or
establish a psychic link (via trump or a spell). Once learned, the form can
readily be assumed. It still cost 15 points of fatigue and takes 45 - skill minutes
to completely change. If you stop concentrating in the middle you revert to your
favorite natural form. Once in the form, you have all the characteristics of the
animal but no instincts to guide you . You are also roughly the same mass so
you'll need a high strength to fly in your large bird form. Without the benefit of
instinct you'll need to learn how to use your new form the hard way: trial and
error.
Automatic Shapeshift (M/A) - this is the ability to "let go" and have your body
adapt to its environment automatically . This is the skill you use when essaying
the Logrus. It is also the best mechanism for surviving the ever-changing
landscape of the Courts and the surrounding shadows. Basically, you cut your
consciousness out of the loop and let your unconscious mind manipulate your
form. This is very good if your being strangled or drowning or otherwise being
damaged. However, it can also be unwelcome . If you automatically shapeshift
then others will know you for what you are and your body's idea for what's good
for you may be rather short sighted. For example, if you suffer a major wound,
your body will go into a super healing mode and move resources away from
unnecessary activities like thinking. If you use this skill often, the GM might rule
that this is your primary state requiring you to make a will roll every time
something might stimulate a shift. For example, you go down to the pool on a
hot August day on the shadow Earth and jump in the water. Before you know it,
you've turn pitch black (to handle the hot sun), grown gills, and have webbing
between your toes and fingers. Try explaining that to your date.
Primal Form (M/A) prereq. Automatic Shapeshifting 13+- this is Automatic
Shapeshifting one step further. In primal form, your are at your most durable
but it requires completely short-circuiting your consciousness. While in this form
you are pure change and fury. You'll do what ever it takes to survive. In order
to recover control, the character may have to wait until the Primal form finds
some place safe where it might nap or it gets into a situation where
shapeshifting and pure fury can't survive. Maybe your friends will have to hunt
down the Primal Form and force it to change.

Note on Lynchpins: Lynchpins serve a dual function. First, they "hold" the spell
to the construct and thus determine the spell's storage life. Second, they make
the spell adaptable to specific conditions at the time it is released. A spell must
take into account the magical laws of the shadow where it's cast and it must be
directed. A spellike Heal Self can have the target (yourself) hard-coded at the
time it is hung but, with a spell like Fireball, you'd like to determine a target at
the time you cast it. By reducing the number of magical phrases that must be
linked in the hanging spell, you extend it's storage life and minimize the
maintenance. The trade off for the flexibility and the longer life is a longer
casting time. I it takes you three second to rattle off the lynchpins for Burning
Death when an enemy can take one step and stab you in the stomach, it is
obvious this spell should not be used for this combat. To state clearly, if you are
interrupted while speaking the lynchpins for a spell, that spell will either not work
or it will misfire.

Shadow Wide (x4) - In a particular shadow, you find the item everywhere you
go, in horde numbers.

Cross-Shadow Environmental (x5) - Found in every one of the infinite number
of shadows with same environment. For example, a warm-water swamp
creature will be sound in every shadow with a warm-water swamp. A limitation
is the need for the item to "blend" in with the environment. If the warm-water
swamp creature is an alligator, then it must blend in with the natural alligators of
each shadow containing alligators. Each shadow will contain about a dozen
items but only one in any given location

Ubiquitous in Shadow (x6) - They're everywhere, they' re everywhere
Anywhere, throughout all shadow, the item is always waiting to be found. The
only exceptions are the poles of existence, Amber and the Courts. The item
must also appear normal and, moreover, commonplace

SHADOWS

Here it is, your chance to create a world. While it is possible, once play starts
to go off and find anything, paying for a shadow guarantees its relationship to
your character. Find what you want in game play can be a little more difficult
The GM may interrupt your desires in a way consistent with the universe but not
at all what you wanted. By buying a shadow, you control it more completely. It
becomes, like an artifact or creature, something a part of your character
Corwin's Avalon is such, an example, or Mandor's Mandorways
To have a shadow requires that the character have power of shadow. The mus
have Pattern or Logrus

Basic Shadow Type

Personal Shadow (5 points) - This gets you whatever you want: a copy of
Shadow Earth but experiencing the eighteenth century , a land of fairies and
elves running around in an environment of very high mana, or a high-tech
universe with a galactic empire and FTL ships
Shadow of the Realm (10 points) - This is a realm with in easy riding distance
of Amber or the Courts of Chaos. It is just outside the Golden Circle or the
Black Zone. While convenient in terms of time flow and travel distance, the
proximity of the shadow to the :"real" Realm, will make the royal family interest
in what happens there.
Primal Plane (20 points) - As with personal shadow, it can be shaped by the

player into anything. The difference being a residual amount of "realness"
leftover from the creation of the Pattern or even pre-dating the Logrus

Shadow Barrier

Communication Barrier (5 points) - the shadow is barred against methods of
reaching across shadow. It can include any or all from the Pattern, the Logrus
Trump, Magic, or Psychic. It can block incoming or outgoing or both. It is not
selective. You can not have "every Trump but mine"
Restricted Access (10 points) access to the shadow is limited in some way. It
could be a geographic feature (only via large mountain range) or O particular
type of feature (through any rug merchant's shop) or something else (the
character must hop on one foot in a circle while whistling)
Guarded (20 points) - a guardian or type of guardian will intercept anyone
entering the shadow. The instructions and capabilities of the guardians are left
up to the creator. Note that specialized guardians may need be purchased as
personalized creatures.

Control of Shadow

Control of Contents (5 points) - the character can manipulate the shadow's
material, creatures, society, history, reality, and physical laws. For example, the
character can decide that magic will cease to function. Or, as another example
the royal family of a kingdom will not have existed and another form of
government will take its place

Control Time FIow (10 points) the character can speed up or slow down the
shadows time rate compared to the one constant, Amber. This is handy if a
character needs days to heal but does what to miss the action back in Amber
He can make it so his week in shadow takes place in 5 minutes in Amber
Control Shadow's Destiny (20 points) a shadow can exert influence over
those who walk through infinite Shadow. A character can then make his
shadow in the destiny of another shadow-walker. By making the shadow part of
Martin's destiny, the next time Martin hellrides, he will find himself in this
shadow.
