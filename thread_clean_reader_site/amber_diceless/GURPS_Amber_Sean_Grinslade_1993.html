GURPS Amber Role-Playing

Amber characters differ from other GURPS characters in important ways. First they are really powerful and hence expensive. Second, beginning characters will be mere novices compared to the elder Amberites and Chaos Lords. Third, players will have a little less control over their characters backgrounds than in normal GURPS adventures. In Amber, players can choose what attributes, powers, and special items they have at the start. They will not be able to choose their parents or their friends. The characters may not even know their parents' or friends' identities.

Another difference is how skills are utilized. Since Amber is a game of mood and perception, having a skill listed on a character sheet does not confer complete understanding. In regular GURPS adventures it is sufficient to say. "I have an electrical engineering skill of 16, so I go in the shop and build a modem." Since much of the Amber universe and the skills that influence it are very subjective, using most skills will be more player intense. For example, Wesley has a high Shadow Manipulation skill and wants to create a pocket shadow. He knows it can be done but has never done it himself. So instead of, "Wesley uses his Shadow Manipulation skill of 18 to create a pocket shadow," the player and game master might interact like:

Player. "Wesley is going to create a pocket shadow." GM: "How? What are you doing?" Player: "What do you mean?" GM: "Is Wesley just standing there thinking? Is he moving? Does he raise the Pattern?" Player: "Oh, yea, Wesley raises the sign of the Pattern before him and..." GM: "Okay, that takes a while and, sure enough, the Pattern hovers before you." Player: "Now, I create a pocket shadow." GM: "How? You are currently just viewing the shadow you're in through the Pattern." Player. "What do I see?" GM: "You note that you seem to be the only real object within the immediate vicinity." Player. "What about in the whole shadow?" GM: “It’ll take about 20 minutes to scan the whole shadow…”

Player: "Forget it, I’ll place my hands in the Pattern and start pulling at the shadow stuff.” GM: "Kinda like a tearing motion?" Player: "Yea. Like this." [the player gestures as if tearing a piece of paper in two.] GM: "You feel the shadow stuff give way. The lighting seems to change. It's getting darker." Player: "What do you mean? Is the sun setting?" GM: "No. It's more like the world is growing dimmer around you. The tear is getting bigger." Player, "Am I still in control of the tear? Can I see anything in it?" GM: "You feel like you have the tear under control. Through the tear it appears dark with flashes of light, like stars." Etc.

Now the character might be on the right track to creating a pocket shadow. Then again he could be destroying the shadow and drawing some attention from a concerned Chaos Lord or Elder Amberite. Once the character has learned to do something, it may not be as involved as this example. However, things are not the same everywhere in the universe of Amber and what you know how to do near Amber may not work near Chaos. Also, it is possible another force is acting against you. Maybe the Logrus was just waiting for a rend in that shadow at that time and will be pumping power through the hole making it larger than what Wesley wants.

When designing an Amber character it is important not to forget that most characters will be aware of their heritage and will have been taught courtly skills. In addition to skills like Fencing, Savoir-Faire, Heraldry (Amber or Courts), and Riding (Horse or Flimsy), don't forget Poetry, History (Amber or Courts), Music, Singing, Bard, Performance, Acting, Dancing, Navigation, Survival (Woodland or Chaos), Seamanship, Tactics, Leadership, Strategy, First-Aid, Area Knowledge (Castle Amber, Kolvir, Courts, Shadow Earth), Bow, Staff, Knife, and Shield. Obvious skills need to survive include Stealth, Carousing, Gambling, Holdout, Camouflage, Driving (Car), and Poison. It is possible that an unknown bastard grew up without influence of his/her Amber parent and just recently came into his/her heritage. Such a character would be at disadvantage in any courtly situation but might make up for it with a set of very well developed skills.

A beginning Amber character is 1571 points. This may seem like a lot but the base-line character package cost 1071. This gives the player 500 points for powers, artifacts, creatures, and skills. Amber characters can also take up to 200 points in disadvantages (if an Elder Amberite is your enemy, it's worth major points). The next sections define how and where you may spend your points.

Basic Amber Package

Amberites are tough people. This is the starting template for all Amber characters. It is possible to lower the value on the attributes or to do away with some native advantages to get more points for Powers or skills. This is not recommended. Remember this is the baseline. Elder Amberites and Chaos Lords have attributes and advantages far beyond these levels.

The Pattern (200 points)

The Pattern is the source of power for Royal Family of Amber. It is Order. It is a Pole of Existence. Without the Pattern this multiverse would not exist. It is all that stands against utter Chaos. No wonder it's a pain to walk it. In game terms, the Pattern Imprint costs 200 points and is a prerequisite for using any of the Pattern skills. To walk the Pattern (or have walked it) requires a HT of 22 or greater and a Will (IQ+Strong Will-Weak Will) of 18 or greater. Beyond these requirements it will only allow those of the blood of Oberon to walk it. If you seek an exception to these rules, take it up with your gamemaster. Trump Artistry (120 points)

Trumps are an eternal link between image and reality. Trump can be used for communication across limitless shadow, or for transportation. The subject of a trump may be a person, creature, place, or thing. A fully invested trump card is very resistant to a all forms of damage and to the influences of the Logrus or Pattern. Trump sketches on the other hand are only as durable as the material they are recorded on. Trumps are unique artifacts that cannot be summoned found in shadow, or conjured. Only a trump artist can create them.

Shapeshifting (80 points)

You have the ability to change your shape. This sounds really cool but it has a couple of caveats. One, it requires tremendous energy to shape shift: 3 points of fatigue for minor things, 10 for major changes in the same form, and 15 points to shift forms completely. Two, some forms requires changes to brain tissues which can cause personality changes. These personality changes can actually lead to the character being trapped in one form obeying some instinctual

agenda and not their own. Three, because shifting is a chaotic manifestation, it is possible to be come infected with Primal Chaos. This chaos cancer can cripple even kill. There are doctors in the Courts who treat these infections but the rumor is that their methods are less that gentle. As a shapeshifter you have three natural forms that you can assume (for 15 points of fatigue) very quickly based on your skill. With practice other forms can become well-known enough to allow changes in a 2-3 minute time frame. When attempting to assume a new form, it can take up to hours to get it right. A general, limited likeness (same general shape, anyway) can be assumed in a 10-20 minute time frame.

The forms are: Human Form: This is the simplest form with no great advantages or disadvantages. For Chaos Lords, it provides the best resistance to the influence of the Pattern. Demon Form: This Chaos manifestation is called demon form because the appearance is similar to the real demons of Chaos. In this form the character can have up to 100 points in advantages added as long as they are bound by increased DR, natural weaponry (claws, fangs horns), or extra limbs (wings). No skills can be increased or attributes raised. It is functionally the same as getting dressed in armor and strapping on weapons. Even Amberite shapeshifters have this form, they may just not be aware of it. Because it is a Chaos manifestation appears in the form near the realms of Order would draw certain attention. Avatar Form: For Amberites, this form is usually some special animal form. In this form, Amberites gain the natural advantages of the animal (movement, fangs, etc.) and maintain their own attributes. For example if Gerard was a shapeshifter he would be a mighty bear with claws and his strength. For Chaosites, it is an elemental form based on air, fire, water, or stone. This form is ideally suited for surviving in the chaotic shadows near the Courts. The form is immune to whatever elemental environment from which it comes but it cannot be assumed or maintained in the realms of Order Shapeshift Animal Form (M/A) - this is the ability to assume the form of some kind of animal. In order to shift, the character observe closely the animal or establish a psychic link (via trump or a spell). Once learned, the form can readily be assumed. It still cost 15 points of fatigue and takes 45 - skill minutes to completely change. If you stop concentrating in the middle you revert to your favorite natural form. Once in the form, you have all the characteristics of the animal but no instincts to guide you . You are also roughly the same mass so you'll need a high strength to fly in your large bird form. Without the benefit of instinct you'll need to learn how to use your new form the hard way: trial and error. Automatic Shapeshift (M/A) - this is the ability to "let go" and have your body adapt to its environment automatically . This is the skill you use when essaying the Logrus. It is also the best mechanism for surviving the ever-changing landscape of the Courts and the surrounding shadows. Basically, you cut your consciousness out of the loop and let your unconscious mind manipulate your form. This is very good if you're being strangled or drowning or otherwise being damaged. However, it can also be unwelcome . If you automatically shapeshift then others will know you for what you are and your body's idea for what's good for you may be rather short sighted. For example, if you suffer a major wound, your body will go into a super healing mode and move resources away from unnecessary activities like thinking. If you use this skill often, the GM might rule that this is your primary state requiring you to make a will roll every time something might stimulate a shift. For example, you go down to the pool on a hot August day on the shadow Earth and jump in the water. Before you know it, you've turn pitch black (to handle the hot sun), grown gills, and have webbing between your toes and fingers. Try explaining that to your date. Primal Form (M/A) prereq. Automatic Shapeshifting 13+- this is Automatic Shapeshifting one step further. In primal form, you are at your most durable but it requires completely short-circuiting your consciousness. While in this form you are pure change and fury. You'll do whatever it takes to survive. In order to recover control, the character may have to wait until the Primal form finds some place safe where it might nap or it gets into a situation where shapeshifting and pure fury can't survive. Maybe your friends will have to hunt down the Primal Form and force it to change. Advanced Shapeshifting skills Shapeshift Aura (M/H) Prereq. All Basic Skills 15+- this is the ability to change your mental structure to "scan" differently . It's not impersonation, more disguise. You can even change to appear like an animal or a plant. Shapeshift Persona (M/H) Prereq Shapeshift Aura 13+- this is the ability to adapt even your personality to be like someone else's. This way even little mannerisms and behaviors won't trip you up when impersonating someone. There is a danger here. If you adopt the personality of a stronger mind, you will lose control and become as if you are the other person. Usually you'll revert once the character has had a good nights rest. Note also that your perceptions of things might change. An easygoing persona may make you take things less seriously than you normally might take them. Or a paranoid persona could make you suspect your best friend. Shapeshift Features (M/H) Prereq. All Basic Skills 15+ - this is the ability to look completely like another person, even if knocked unconscious or sleeping. It requires close contact and practice to achieve the best results,. If the person is

either much larger or smaller than you are, you will appear as a skinny or fat version of the person, After about a week in the form and you'll gain or lose the necessary mass. Shapeshift Internal Structures (WH) Prereq. All Basic Skills 15+- this is the ability to rearrange of modify your internal structures . Beyond the natural advantages of having two hearts (or one in a different place) or a sub-brain, this is also the skill to use to regenerate . About a week 0 constant use wil regrow a limb or organ. Shapeshift to Creature of Power (M/VH) Prereq. Shapeshift Internal Structures 15+ - with this skill you can assume the form of a creature of power complete with skills and abilities. For example, you could become a dragon or a true demon. You must have the opportunity to study the creature in detail before being able to assume its form. Note also that the creatures mind may be significantly alien thereby displacing your own consciousness or heavily influencing it. Magic

GURPS magic works the same, more or less, with some important differences. Mana is the ambient, background energy resulting from the wave flux between the Pattern and the Logrus. It is simply shadow energy. In a shadow where the mana is normal, only mages (those with the magery advantage) can cast spells. Spells are complex mental constructs used to channel mana into desired changes. Where the mana is high, anyone learning a spell can cast it. Very high mana shadows will warp and twisted at the promptings of a strong wisher, who needs no spells, just desire and imagination. Likewise shadows with less mana make casting more difficult if not impossible. Okay, now here's the catch, when you learn an ordinary spell it only works in one shadow! You will have to re-learn the same spell (often with different rituals) in each shadow. This also

means all prerequisite spells must be re-learned. Imagine a character with a spell list like: Fireball(Earth)(M/H)-18 Fireball(Avalon)(M/H)-15 Fireball(Google-Plex)(M/H)-10 Fireball(Oz) (M/H) -20 Shadow wizards have it tough. For the Lords of the Universe, those with access to Real Power (that is, the Pattern or the Logrus) there is an alternative. By using disciplines that are something like Engineering, Philosophy, Art Appreciation, and Research an Amber character can create or devise spells that will work in most any shadow. Because the spells created in this fashion are so complex that casting them takes anywhere from 20 minutes to hours, true sorcerers partially cast their spells into constructs of power omitting a few key phrases that will trigger it when needed, The obvious constructs to uses for "hanging" spells are the Pattern or the Logrus which can reach and provide power in almost any shadow. It is possible to use other devices or living things to store spells. The disadvantages to using such complicated magic is the maintenance. Spells, once hung must be refreshed each week (or sooner) or they deteriorate. This is why most elder Amberites don't use spells. How would you like to spend 20 hours a week doing repetitive, tiring work for a few lousy spells over the course of a century? Besides to speak each lynchpin (key words omitted when the spell was hung) takes at least a second making it faster to stick a sword through someone instead of parboiling them with a spell. Outside of actual spells, Amberites and Chaosites can learn Power Words that, when spoken, affect an immediate, if very temporary, effect. Since these can be spoken in less than a second they are ideal for combat. The disadvantages are they are hard to learn (beginning characters can have a max. of five), they are based on the IQ of the speaker limiting their power, and they last but an instant.
