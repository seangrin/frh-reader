Advanced Character Design Evolution

Autafon “Amber” Deliamber

These versions represent early design iterations of Amber prior to player selection. They are mechanically valid but would require tuning before being reintroduced into the current campaign.

They illustrate how a character’s method of applying force can shift campaign tone without altering core identity.

1. Dragon Breath Version

This version is the most overtly cinematic.

Core Identity

  • DX 17 / IQ 17

  • Judo 17

  • Guns (Pistol) 19

  • Psionic Healing 18

  • Telepathy 5

  • ESP 5

  • Dragon’s Breath 16 (5d fire, 3/day)

Page 1 makes it clear: this Amber can walk into a warehouse and resolve the situation.

What This Version Does Well

  • Clear combat fallback.

  • Flashy supernatural hook.

  • Strong medical backbone (Diagnosis 17, Physician 17).

  • Strong social read tools (Empathy, Detect Lies 18*).

Design Implication

Dragon’s Breath alters campaign tone.

In a 1923 investigative horror framework, a 5d flame jet at range 50 is:

  • Spectacular

  • Loud

  • Reputation-altering

  • Campaign-shifting

It tilts the game toward pulp action.

This Amber feels like:
Lotus Bell enforcer who can also heal.

2. Martial Artist Version

This version emphasizes controlled lethality.

Notable Differences

  • Judo 18 (Parry 13)

  • Pressure Points 17

  • Pressure Secrets 15

  • Hit Location (Eye) techniques

  • Power Blow

  • Blinding Touch

Dragon’s Breath is removed.

Now she is:

A disciplined close-quarters specialist built for controlled lethality.

She retains:

  • Psionic Healing 18

  • Telepathy 5

  • ESP 5

Tone Effect

This version:

  • Fits investigative horror tone more cleanly.

  • Keeps violence intimate.

  • Maintains plausible deniability.

  • Is frightening without spectacle.

This integrates cleanly with FRH’s “consequences matter” ethos.

3. Psi Version ( Selection Basis)

This is the conceptual direction that evolved into the Amber.

What Changed

  • IQ 18

  • DX 16 (reduced physical dominance)

  • Astral Projection 3

  • Telepathy 6

  • Strong Will 21

  • Psionic Healing 18

  • Expanded perception and control suite

  • Reduced martial specialization

Identity Shift

She becomes:

Psychic intelligence officer + healer + institutional operator.

This version:

  • Controls tempo

  • Extracts information

  • Stabilizes the team

  • Escalates lore carefully

  • Enables others without dominating spotlight

It aligns best with FRH’s tone and table dynamics.

Design Continuity

Across all versions:

  • Patron (Followers of the Right Hand)

  • Secret (Lotus Bell)

  • Minority Group disadvantage

  • Pacifism (Cannot Harm Innocents)

  • Strong Will

  • High medical competency

  • Awareness + Empathy core

  • Unnatural Feature (Eyes Glow Green)

Amber’s spine never changed.
Only her method of applying force evolved.
The character concept remained constant; only the vector of power shifted.

Table Note

Ashley ultimately chose the psychic healer direction.
She also could not say “Autafon,” and the character became known simply as Amber.

Why This Appendix Exists

This folder exists to show:

  • Power scaling choices affect tone.

  • Small mechanical adjustments can shift genre.

  • Character identity is deeper than combat role.

  • builds are the result of deliberate tuning.

These drafts are mechanically playable but would require adjustment for current campaign balance.

Why This Is Valuable

This appendix quietly teaches your players:

  • How to think about builds.

  • How tone emerges from mechanics.

  • That you’re not winging balance.

  • That iteration is normal.

It encourages thoughtful character construction and tone awareness at the table.
