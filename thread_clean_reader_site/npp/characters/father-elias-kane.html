Description

Father Elias Kane was a promising attorney before the law ceased to feel large enough for the kinds of harm he could already see. He entered holy orders, served in the Great War, and came back with steadier hands, worse dreams, and less patience for pious fraud.

He was later removed from ordinary parish service after a failed exorcism and a refusal to bury what powerful men wanted buried. He is still ordained. That is part of the problem.

Elias is the sort of priest who can talk a rector into lending an altar stone, a clerk into opening a file, and a bruiser into telling the truth. When evil is near, he becomes very still.

GM Notes

This is a competency-porn priest, not a helpless theologian. He should be able to win early-case scenes through discernment, Church access, records work, and calm force of presence.

The real tension is not whether faith exists. It is whether he is still fit to carry it cleanly.

His best party niche is social-investigative authority with one genuinely frightening holy intervention when a malign being presses too close.
