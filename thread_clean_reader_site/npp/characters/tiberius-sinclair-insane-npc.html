GM NPC Tool — Broken State. This sheet documents what Tiberius Sinclair looks like when the laudanum, the telepathy, and whatever entered his mind from the direction of deep water have finished their work. 25 points. The powers are still there. He no longer believes they are.

Tiberius Sinclair

Insane NPC State

The glamour is still effort. But now the effort is all going to the tidal coordinates he mutters under his breath, the life preserver he sleeps in, and the absolute certainty that the sea is watching through the walls — and that his gifts, such as they were, are gone.

Points: 25 Age: 30 NPC Status: This is not a playable character. It is a GM encounter state — what the campaign finds if it looks for Tiberius in the wrong places at the wrong time. Powers — Present but Unavailable

  • Telepathy 6 [36] (Instantaneous: +20%) — still active, believed gone: the full telepathic suite — Telereceive-17, Telescan-16, Suggest-17, Sleep-15, Mental Blow-15, Mental Stab-15, and all the rest — is mechanically intact. The Delusion (Cannot use TK and Telepathy) means Tiberius no longer attempts to deploy it. He believes it is gone. Whether it is truly gone, merely suppressed, or operating below his awareness — leaking into his phobias and delusions in ways he cannot identify — is a GM question the campaign can open.
  • Telekinesis 5 [20] — same: the fine-manipulation capacity is still there. He does not reach for it. He may not even recognize accidental manifestations.
  • ESP 5 (no skill, powers Danger Sense) [0]: Danger Sense [15] is still listed as an advantage. Whether it functions through the Delusion's suppression is ambiguous. He may sense danger and interpret it as paranoia rather than warning.
  • IQ 17, Detect Lies, Psychology-17: the intellectual capability that made him an effective investigator is intact. It is now directed almost entirely inward, toward the management of phobia thresholds and the maintenance of fractured identity rather than toward cases.

FRH Package — Degraded

The FRH institutional affiliations are technically still present. Whether they remain operationally functional when the person holding them has Combat Paralysis, Pacifism (Total), Cowardice, and a Fear of Psionic Powers is the question.

  • Patron (Followers of the Right Hand) (15 or less) [60]: still listed. FRH may have a rescue, recovery, or containment interest in this state.
  • Enemy (Unknown Enemies of FRH) [-60]: present. The enemy does not care about Tiberius's current condition. This NPC state creates a vulnerability that the enemy can exploit — he cannot protect himself, and he cannot be usefully deployed against them.
  • Note: Combat Reflexes is absent in this state. It was present in the PC version. Its loss is part of what marks this as a degraded form.

Required Secret Power — Inverted

In the PC version, the hidden psionic gifts are the secret. In this NPC state, the secret has become the opposite: Tiberius's Delusion (Cannot use TK and Telepathy) means the powers remain hidden — but now hidden from him, rather than by him.

The relevant mechanical cluster:

  • Telepathy 6 [36] and Telekinesis 5 [20] — mechanically present
  • Delusion (Cannot use TK and Telepathy) [-15] — he believes these are gone
  • Fear of Psionic Powers (Mild) [-10] — he fears his own gifts even in their suppressed state
  • Secret (Psionic powers) [-30] — still applies; the powers are still hidden from the world even though he no longer believes he has them

Whether recovery is possible — whether the Delusion can be broken, the fear addressed, and the gifts reclaimed — is the campaign thread this NPC state opens.

Required Secret Society

Patron (Secret Society) (9 or less) [5]: Secret: -5.

As in the PC version, the source lists this as a literal placeholder — "Patron (Secret Society)" — with no actual organization named. The society's identity is not recorded in the source for either the PC or NPC sheet.

In this NPC state, the unnamed society may have an interest in Tiberius's condition: as asset to be recovered, liability to be managed, or subject of whatever purpose the society was pursuing through him. That angle is only available once the society is named.

FRH does not satisfy the secret-society requirement.

How This State Functions in Play

  • The phobia cluster defines the encounter geography. Tiberius cannot enter spaces with yellow curtains (Severe), rooms near ocean sound (Fear of Oceans — Severe), or libraries and archives (Phobia: Books — Severe, reinforced by Compulsive Behavior: Destroys Written Text). Finding him means finding the geography of his avoidance.
  • The Delusion (The Sea Watches Through Walls) combined with the ocean phobia and cephalopod phobia suggests something specific entered his telepathic field — a coastal, deep-water, or entity-adjacent experience that the laudanum and the volume of psychic input combined to make permanent. The GM can choose to make this specific (a named encounter he cannot remember clearly) or atmospheric.
  • Split Personality [-15] means the Wraith, the socialite, and whatever the breakdown created are not stably integrated. Under pressure, different aspects can surface. The investigator who is still inside him — IQ 17, Psychology-17, Detect Lies-18 — can sometimes be reached.
  • Pacifism (Total) [-30] and Combat Paralysis [-15] mean this version of Tiberius will not fight under any circumstances and will freeze in physical danger. He is not a combatant and cannot be made into one.
  • Compulsive Behavior (Destroys Written Text) [-15] means any written material in his vicinity is at risk. His Phobia (Books) is severe. He cannot be in a library. Investigation work that requires archives is closed to him entirely.
  • The Filthy Rich [50] infrastructure is still present. The wealth exists. The Sinclair name still opens doors. The house is still standing and the accounts are still funded. This is either a resource the party can use — finding him through his financial trail — or evidence of how completely the breakdown is hidden from the public-facing world.

Why This State Works

The insane NPC Tiberius is useful in FRH because it gives the campaign a visible version of the failure mode the PC was working against. He was trying to remain himself before the night, the secrets, or the chemicals decided to define him first. This is what it looks like when that contest is lost. The wealth is still there. The gifts are still there. The person who can use them is not — or not yet.

Character Overview

Tiberius Sinclair was trying to remain himself before the night, the secrets, or the chemicals decided to define him first. He did not entirely succeed. The man in this state still carries every advantage the Wraith required: the wealth, the gifts, the trained skill set, the IQ 17 that made him a devastating investigator in rooms where other people were lying. None of it is gone. What is gone is the functional architecture that allowed him to deploy it.

The breakdown has a specific texture. The tidal coordinates he mutters, the life preserver he sleeps in, the certainty that the sea is watching through walls, the severe phobia of cephalopods and yellow curtains — this is not generic madness. It is the residue of something specific that entered his telepathic field and did not leave. A powerful enough psychic contact with a mind that thinks in ocean terms, in deep-water geometry, in the tidal patience of something very old and very alien — that is one explanation. The campaign can confirm it, complicate it, or leave it open. The important thing is that it does not feel random. Every phobia in this cluster points in the same direction.

What makes him useful as a GM NPC is precisely the gap between what he was and what he is. The party that encounters him in this state inherits the question: is this recoverable? At what cost? Who did this to him, or what did he encounter that left these marks? And if the Delusion (Cannot use TK and Telepathy) were broken — if the telepathy came back online in someone who currently has no way to manage it, no Combat Reflexes, no operative's edge — what would that look like?

The Filthy Rich infrastructure means he is findable. The Sinclair name means discovery creates institutional pressure. The FRH Enemy means someone, somewhere, is aware that the Wraith is down and may be interested in using that fact. The Secret Identity (The Wraith) remains intact — which means whoever or whatever broke him either did not know who he was, or knew and chose to leave the wreckage in place rather than expose it.
