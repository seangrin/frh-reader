Reading Protocol

Mission

Read transcript segments in a way that preserves training signal.

Segment Unit

The basic training unit is one 30-minute segment Markdown file.

Each segment contains: - YAML front matter - stable line IDs - scene markers when available - additive NPC overlays

How To Read

  1. Read the front matter first.
  2. Note the session, segment, time window, and active NPC overlays.
  3. Read the transcript from top to bottom without skipping.
  4. Track lines by ID.
  5. Notice shifts between:
    • GM adjudication
    • GM framing
    • NPC embodiment
    • player banter
    • OOC table culture
    • rules / mechanics discussion

How To Think

While reading, ask:

  • What is Sean doing here as a GM?
  • Is Sean applying pressure, permissiveness, misdirection, or clarification?
  • Is an NPC carrying exposition, threat, etiquette, or temptation?
  • Is the table joking, destabilizing, recovering, or clarifying?
  • What is being taught by the social consequences of play?

What Not To Do

  • Do not rewrite the segment into prose.
  • Do not trim banter away.
  • Do not reduce the segment to bullet highlights before reading it.
  • Do not assume a character voice is a player voice.
  • Do not assume an NPC overlay is present on every line of a long NPC block; use surrounding context too.

Quote Method

Use line IDs when citing.

Example: - At S03-04-099, Sean uses future-tech banter to soften the opening before pressure resumes. - At S01-05-279 through S01-05-292, Reagan becomes a social-consequence anchor.
