Ambrose Augustus Auggie Fontaine

Cultivated occult aspirant, social hinge, and emotional center.

Auggie arrives as a man of performance, cultivation, and appetite for deeper power. He is stylish, theatrical, and socially deft, but beneath that polish is a very real hunger to become more than a dilettante. Across the campaign he becomes the nearest thing the party has to a center of gravity: the one who keeps people talking, steadies chaotic scenes, and increasingly acts like someone built to carry mysteries that might ruin other people.

Autafon Amber Deliamber

Healer, aura-reader, record-keeper, and quiet truth-anchor.

Amber is one of the campaign's least flashy presences and one of its most essential. She reads what other people miss, heals what others cannot, and keeps becoming the person whose perception quietly determines what the party can actually know. Her authority is not loud or domineering. It comes from being consistently precise, useful, and morally centered in a campaign full of compromised men, unstable egos, and overlapping lies.

Crispin Pollick

Assassin, ghost-walker, social predator, and the campaign's cold edge.

Crispin is what happens when operational competence, criminal instinct, and post-human hunger get wrapped into one person. He is dangerous long before combat starts. He thinks in routes, leverage, weakness, and exits. Yet he is never just a blunt weapon. Some of the campaign's best saves come from him reading a room correctly, improvising the right lie, or recognizing that somebody else's panic needs to be managed before his own power matters.

Matthew Bridges

Gifted unstable prodigy, wealthy heir, and clue-magnet.

Matthew is brilliant, insecure, rich, impulsive, and unusually permeable to the setting's deeper currents. He has genuine gifts and often grasps at meaning faster than he can emotionally hold it. That instability makes him funny at the table, but it also makes him deeply sympathetic. He wants significance, respect, and belonging, and the campaign keeps giving him pieces of all three in ways that threaten to crack him open rather than reassure him.

Preston Henderson

Infernal professional, charming coward, and cosmology insider.

Preston joins later, but once he arrives he changes the campaign's register immediately. He knows too much about Hell, bargains, and damned logistics to be merely another occultist. At the same time, he is not a fearless dark knight. He is frightened, vain, curious, and often tragically funny, which makes him fit the party perfectly. He brings infernal literacy without losing the human awkwardness that keeps the game from becoming pompous.

How the Party Works

The group works because no one is clean and no one is redundant. Auggie holds social and emotional cohesion, Amber stabilizes knowledge and care, Crispin turns panic into tactics, Matthew magnetizes clues and instability, and Preston widens the metaphysical field while keeping the whole thing funny and dangerous. FRH reads best when you think of them not as roles in a balanced party, but as different answers to the same question: what kind of person keeps making choices in a universe that will never fully explain itself?
