Tiberius Example Play-Through

Example of Play

The Wraith on 59th Street

This example demonstrates: Shadowing, Stealth, Telepathy, Telecontrol, Sleep, grappling, surprise bonuses, mental powers during combat, encumbrance, and tactical lethality.

In this example, Tiberius (in his identity of The Wraith) will primarily rely on the following abilities: Acting-12 (default from IQ; B174), Combat Reflexes, Fast-Draw-16, First Aid-16, Mental Blow-16, Shadowing-16, Sleep-15, Stealth-15, Telereceive-17, and Telecontrol-17. Skills on the character sheet are listed in alphabetical order for ease of lookup during play. GURPS source books referenced: Basic Set, 3rd Edition (Revised); Psionics; Martial Arts.


Note: “B###” = Basic Set, “P###” = Psionics, “MA p.#” = Martial Arts.

Scene Setup

Location: Outside a cafe across from the 59th Street Theater
Time: 10 PM
Objective: Track Tony Marks, suspected henchman of Mario Botalli
Stakes: The kidnapped Jackson boy may be nearby

Tiberius Sinclair is in disguise, leaning casually against a wall, cigarette lit, watching the theater exit. He carries his usual Wraith kit.

Street Surveillance

Game Master (GM): You are leaning against the wall outside a cafe across the street from the 59th Street Theater. It is around 10 PM and you expect Tony Marks to come out anytime. According to the cop who works for your Patron, Tony works for Mario Botalli who could be behind the Jackson kid’s kidnapping.

Tiberius (T): Great. Now, I am carrying my usual Wraith kit (lockpick set, grapple, rope, 4 small knives, 1 large knife, rope garrote, .38 Special, sword cane, 2 sets of handcuffs, compass, binoculars, bladed knuckles, individual first aid kit) with my outer Wraith cloak and my big hat. I used my disguise kit so I look like just some ordinary well-off gentleman. I keep smoking a cigarette and watch the front of the theater.

GM: Gotcha. In a few minutes, Tony comes out and starts heading east toward the river. He is smiling, but moving fast.

T: Can I read his mind from here?

GM: He’s across the street heading away from you so about 10 to 15 ft outside your range.

T: OK, I follow him, being careful to not be noticed.

GM: Roll against your Shadowing skill (T: Shadowing-16, B67) and tell me how much you succeed or fail by.

T: (rolls 3d6, gets 11) OK, so I made it by 4.

GM: (rolls some dice, checks some papers, makes a weird face) Great, as far as you can tell, he has not noticed you following him through the crowd.

Following Tony

T: Noice!

GM: He continues east toward the river. The crowd thins as the buildings change from shops and homes to warehouses. If you want to continue to follow unnoticed, you’ll have to use Stealth (T: Stealth-15, B64). Roll against your Stealth.

T: (rolls 14) Ah, made it by 1.

GM: (rolls some dice, shuffles papers) He doesn’t appear to notice you. After a few minutes, he stops at a side door of a warehouse right on the East River. Reaching into his pockets…

T: Does my Danger Sense go off! I get ready to fast draw my gun if he pulls a gun on me!

GM: …no, he pulls out some keys and starts unlocking the door.

T: Whew! OK, can I sneak close enough to read his mind before he goes in?

GM: Sure, you can try. From the alley you are in now, there is one next to the warehouse Tony is at. Roll against Stealth to see if you can sneak over there.

T: (rolls 9) Made it by 5.

GM: Tony continues to work on getting the door open and doesn’t appear to notice you.

Reading Tony's Mind

T: OK, I use Telereceive (T: Telereceive-17, P25) to read his thoughts. Remember, I don’t have to concentrate for a turn, since my Telepathy is instantaneous. (rolls 3d6) I get 11 making it by 6.

GM: (Consults some papers, rolls some dice) Wow, well, since poor Tony isn’t special in any way, your success by 6 means you have access to all surface thoughts, mental associations, and all his senses. You are also “in his mind” for the purposes of Telecontrol, Suggest, and Sleep. You were also subtle enough he didn’t even experience any mental invasion.

T: What is Tony thinking?

GM: Tony is thinking:

“Man, I hope they come through with the ransom soon. (GM note: Mental Association here with $10,000) I mean, I like the kid (GM note: MA Tony pictures an image of Pauly Jackson tied to a chair in a dark room) But, I wanna spend the night at my girlfriend’s place and not babysitting a crybaby we’re going to whack anyway (GM note: MA image of a handgun).”

Tony is also thinking about the 3 guys upstairs playing cards outside the room where the boy is tied up. He’s got to relieve Guido and spend the next 10 hours trying to stay awake. Tony wasn’t supposed to party before guarding the kid but it’s boring duty.

Taking Control

T: I think I found the Jackson boy. First things first, I use Telecontrol (T: Telecontrol-17, P29) to take over Tony. (rolls 3d6) I got a 13 out of (*sucks air through teeth*) 14, so I made it by only 1?

GM: (rolls some dice) Hell, Tony completely failed his will roll, so you have a Tony puppet. What are you going to do?

T: What does he see directly in front of him?

GM: He is looking into a short, straight hallway (about 30 ft) that leads to a stairwell leading up. There is a standard interior door in the middle of the left-hand wall in about 12 or so ft.

T: No people?

GM: No people, no pets, just a plain ugly white painted hallway.

T: I have Tony walk into the hallway just far enough so I can race from my hiding spot and close the door behind me.

GM: I assume you are going to sneak into the doorway?

T: Does anyone see me?

GM: Not that you can tell.

T: Yes, I sneak up to the door, get in behind Tony, and close the door. (rolls 3d6) I made my
Stealth by 2.

GM: OK. What do you do now?

T: I listen carefully to see if anyone is shouting an alarm or otherwise seems to have noticed me. Is my Danger Sense going off?

GM: No, you are not getting any preternatural sense of danger. Roll against your IQ-4 (due to Telecontrol; see P29) to see if you sense any alarm.

T: (rolls 3d6) With an IQ of 17, I rolled a 5; minus 4… so I make it by 1.

GM: You don’t hear anything that sounds like an alert or yelling or ringing.

Inside the Warehouse

T: I get out my pistol and I have Tony walk up the stairs...What do I see up the stairs?

GM: The stairs lead straight up to an interior door with a glass panel that reads, “Office.”

T: OK, I have Tony walk up the stairs slow and I creep behind him, staying about 6 ft back, as we go up.

GM: OK, after a few seconds, Tony reaches the door.

T: Can Tony see through the glass?

GM: Not really. It’s frosted glass. He can see there is a light on beyond the door and it looks like it might open into a hallway.

T: I have Tony try to open the door. I also do a quick check behind me.

GM: Tony opens the door with no problem. Beyond it is a hallway about 30 ft long ending in another glass paneled door. Behind you, the door to the street is still closed and nothing appears behind you.

T: I have Tony continue into the upper hallway about halfway and I follow, crouching, and close the door at the top of the stairs behind me.

GM: OK.

T: I stop and I listen. Can I tell if something is on the other side of that door? Where is this Guido Tony was going to relieve?

GM: Roll against IQ for a sense check, but at -4 since you are using Telecontrol. (-4 is the campaign’s quick adjudication for divided attention while maintaining Telecontrol; see P29)

T: (rolls 3d6) I made it by 5-4 = 1.

GM: The room at the end of this upper hallway has light coming from it. You also hear music from a radio, men talking low, and the smell of cigars.

T: OK, now we’re getting somewhere. Oh shit, I check Tony. Is he packing?

GM: Do you pat him down while you hang out in this hallway at 10:30 at night?

T: Yes!

GM: OK, you’ll need to roll Telecontrol to keep Tony.

T: (rolls 3d6) I make it by 3.

GM: (rolls some dice) You still control Tony and he is carrying a .38 Special and a straight razor.

T: I take those from him.

GM: What do you do with them?

T: I put the razor in my pocket and I put his .38 Special in my shoulder holster while I have my gun in my hand.

GM: Alright, what’s next?

T: I have Tony walk to the door but NOT open it, just stand there while I sneak up behind him. I stay about 3 ft back. What can I hear now?

GM: Roll IQ-4 (due to Telecontrol; see P29)

T: (rolls 3d6) I roll 16, so 17 minus 4 is 13...I missed it by 3. Shit!

GM: You just hear men talking and the smell of cigars.

T: OK, I have my pistol ready. Can I have Tony open the door and look while I crouch behind him unseen? But, I want to be able to shoot into the room, if I want to.

GM: The door does open inward, so that could work. Your Stealth check will be at -4 due to Telecontrol.

T: Shoot. OK, Can I hide in the hallway a bit without a Stealth roll while I have Tony open the door and look in?

GM: Sure. Is that what you want to do?

T: Yes, I have Tony open the door about ¾ and step in a bit to look around while I hide about halfway down the hallway with my pistol ready.

GM: Excellent, now roll to maintain Telecontrol on Tony. (rolls some dice)

T: (rolls 3d6) Rolling 12 against 14, I make it by 2.

The Card Room

GM: Alright, Tony opens the door and looks in. He sees a roughly 15’ x 15’ room with a naked light bulb hanging in the middle over a round wooden table. Around the table are 3 men in suits smoking cigars and playing cards. You recognize Guido from Tony’s earlier impression. He is the big one, about 6’ 2” and built like a truck. The other two look more like accountants with obvious shoulder holsters.

As the door opens, Guido says, “Hey, Tony! Where the hell you been? You’re late!” And, he starts to get up from the opposite side of the table to approach Tony.

T: I have Tony say, “Guido, come out here to the hallway with me.” Then, I have Tony turn around and walk a little bit toward me.

GM: OK. You hear, through both your ears and Tony’s ears, Guido say, “OK, Tony, whatever you say.”

T: Cool, cool, cool. Frick! OK, can I put Tony to Sleep after Guido enters the hallway a bit, out of the view of the door, and still be able to grab him from behind?

GM: Yes, since your Telepathy power is Instantaneous, but to grapple or get a hold, you’ll need both hands free.

T: And grapple uses DX and not ST, right?

GM: Correct. And, you’ll be at +3 plus another +3 for surprise (B122) applied to a grapple attempt (B123) for a total of +6. Provided, of course, your roll to put Tony to Sleep works. (*evil grin*)

Grappling Guido

T: Great. I drop my pistol in my coat pocket. So, when Guido enters the hall a bit, I’ll drop Telecontrol (losing the -4), use Sleep on Tony, and then grapple Guido from behind trying to get position for a choke hold.

GM: Tony walks slowly into the hallway and Guido follows, saying, “Hey, Tony, what’s up?”

T: I drop Telecontrol and Sleep (T: Sleep-15, P31) Tony! (rolls 3d6) I get a 9 against 15 so made it by 6!

GM: (rolls some dice) Tony slumps to the floor, snoozing. Guido rushes forward saying, “Tony, what’s wrong?” You hear some chairs moving in the other room.

T: Oh, great. I jump Guido from behind, so my DX is 15 + 6 is 21 (rolls 3d6) I get 11, made it by 10! Eat that, Guido!

GM: (rolls some dice) You have grappled Guido from behind. The way things went down, your back is to the stairwell door and you and Guido are facing the card-playing room. Grappled, Guido can’t move until he breaks free or you release him (see Grappling, B123). Next turn, you can continue to hold; try a takedown; or choke or strangle (all ST contest); or use Judo for a Choke Hold attempt (MA p.69, Choke Hold).

T: Alright. Are those two guys coming out of the room? Is Guido making any noise?

GM: So far, no one has appeared in the doorway and your Danger Sense has not gone off. Guido is mostly grunting and cursing under his breath, saying, “Who da FUCK are you?!”

T: I try to Telereceive Guido’s thoughts. (rolls 3d6) I roll 5 against 17 so I make it by 12! How deep do I get?

GM: (rolls some dice) (*whistles through his teeth*) Oooh-kay, you see deep into his subconscious including his memories going back over the last week. Everything, his neuroses, his wank-off sessions, how he beats his girlfriends, how he has boyfriends, and how he can’t wait to kill the boy in the back room, Pauly Jackson, slowly, with torture.

T: Bastard! Do I know who else is in the other room?

GM: Yes, the other two guys are wannabe gangster, distant cousins of the warehouse owner. Their guns aren’t even loaded and they don’t know that. Guido is struggling to get free, roll against your ST (rolls some dice)

T: (rolls 3d6) I got 8 against my ST of 12, so I made it by 4. (Quick Contest of ST; B348)

GM: Guido tries to break free but is unable to do so. He gets ready to start yelling.

Clearing the Room

T: Before that happens, since I am already in his mind, I put him to Sleep. (rolls 3d6) I made it by 2.

GM: (rolls some dice) Guido slumps to the floor.

T: I don’t want those guys running away so I try to croak out a, “Help me,” trying to sound like Guido.

GM: Roll versus Acting (T: Acting-12 default, B174) + 2 since it’s just a croak.

T: I don’t have Acting.

GM: OK, roll versus IQ-5 (default if no Acting skill) + 2.

T: So, that is 17 minus 5 is 12, plus 2 = 14 (rolls 3d6), I get 12, I made it by 2.

GM: (rolls some dice) You hear, “Guido, are you alright? We almost there!”

T: As soon as the first one comes through the door, I will Mental Blow him, and save my physical attack for when the next one appears.

GM: Alright. A 5’ 6” Italian gentleman comes through the door with one almost identical behind him. They both look to be in their early 20s.

T: Mental Blow (T: Mental Blow-16, P28) the first dude (rolls 3d6) 14, just made it by 1.

GM: (rolls some dice) OK, the first guy fails his HT roll and is standing there confused, mentally stunned. Subtract one Fatigue (see Fatigue B130; Mental Blow cost P28) from your character. This causes the second to stop and ask, “Siggy, what’s wrong?” (rolls some dice) He doesn’t appear to see you at all.

T: (marks character sheet) While the second guy is worrying, I’ll Telereceive his thoughts. (rolls 3d6) 9 on 17, so by 8.

GM: (rolls some dice) OK, you are into Robert’s subconscious and know his current thoughts but not his memories. (rolls some dice) Siggy remains stunned.

T: Does Robert know where the kid is?

GM: Yes, just beyond the room with the card table, is another small storage room with Pauly tied up to a chair.

T: OK, I Sleep Robert (rolls 3d6) I made it by 1. Did that work?

GM: (rolls some dice) Yes, Robert falls asleep. (rolls some dice) Siggy remains stunned.

T: Do I have any way to quietly drop Siggy without using my Sleep again? Like, do I have chloroform or some of my laudanum?

GM: Sure, you have a couple of doses of laudanum on you.

T: OK, I go to Siggy and try to give him some.

GM: He has to drink it.

T: Damn. Forget it, I Telereceive Siggy while he’s stunned. That’s got to be easier, right? (rolls 3d6) I made it by 4.

GM: Yes, it is a bit easier (rolls some dice), you get into his confused surface thoughts.

T: Don’t care about those, Sleep (rolls 3d6) made it by 3.

GM: (rolls some dice) Siggy slumps next to Robert.

Finding Pauly

T: OK, I quickly check all the gangsters to make sure they are asleep and then I head for the storage room where the kid is located.

GM: They are all snoring. Are you going to use Stealth?

T: Yes (rolls 3d6) Shit, I rolled a 16, I missed it by one.

GM: (rolls some dice) As you make your way through the room with the card table, you accidentally hook a chair with your foot making a small scraping noise as you get to the storage room door.

T: Shoot. I check the door, is it locked?

GM: The door knob is not locked.

T: I open it slowly and look inside. What do I see?

GM: It is a roughly 8’ x 8’ room, the only light comes from the crack in the door you are opening. You see some buckets, mops, brooms, dust bins, and a 12-year-old boy in torn clothes tied to a wooden chair in the middle of the room. His head hangs down, unmoving.

T: I step inside, narrow the door behind me, and use First Aid (T: First Aid-16, B195) to check the kid (rolls 3d6) I rolled 14 and I have a 16 in First Aid. Does my individual first aid kit help?

Danger Sense and Alfred

GM: The kit gives you a +1 so you made it by 3. Pauly is in bad shape but is alive. He needs a physician and medicine, not just first aid. (rolls some dice) Your Danger Sense goes off as you literally feel danger behind you! Roll initiative on 1d6 (Initiative, B122; Combat Reflexes, B43).

T: I have Combat Reflexes so I get +1, I roll a 3, so I have 4 for initiative. I spin around and Fast-Draw (T: Fast-Draw-16, B194) my pistol (rolls 3d6) made my Fast-Draw by 2.

GM: OK, you are not surprised, like you ever are, and go first. When you spin around, you see a medium sized man in a brown suit pointing a pistol at you.

T: Out-of-game: Hey, GM, with instantaneous Telepathy, I can do one psionic power a turn, AND do a regular action on that same turn, right? So I could Telereceive into this guy while attacking him in the same turn?

GM: Yes, that is correct. You cannot do more than one psionic power per turn (see Instantaneous Enhancement, Psionics p.27). You can do more than one at a time by starting them separately, but that’s something different. With instantaneous, you can then do a full turn of regular actions. Is that what you wanna do?

T: Yes, I Telereceive (rolls 3d6) made it by 4 and then I want to shoot him in the throat. What chance would that be? My pistol skill is 16 and the gun SS (Snap Shot number; B115) is 10.

GM: (rolls some dice) OK, the Telereceive works getting only surface thoughts. “Say Hello to Alfred who wants to murder you!” (GM joking). Just to clarify, when you did the Fast-Draw you did so from your shoulder holster, right?

T: Oh, yeah, that’s Tony’s gun?

GM: Yes, but, it’s a .38 Special, so the SS is still 10. OK, so the range is 6 ft (+0), Alfred isn’t moving (+0), and the neck is -5, meaning a target skill level of 11 which is above the SS, so no -4 penalty. So, yeah, 11 or less to shoot Alfred in the throat.

T: I’ll go for it. (rolls 3d6) I got a 9!

GM: Groovy, roll for damage, so that is 2d-1 piercing damage.

T: (rolls 2d) I got a 5 minus 1 is 4 points of damage.

GM: Alright, bullet damage to the human neck causes 2x damage (Neck hit location, B248; see also Parts of the Body table, B203) so that jumps up to 8. Since that is greater than 1/3 of his HT, he must roll to avoid a crushed throat (rolls some dice), his throat is crushed and he will lose 1 HT per second until he dies or gets medical care. He just gurgles, grabs at his neck, and collapses to the floor. You are very lucky you weren’t deeper in his mind, or you’d have to roll to resist the mental shock of a bullet to the windpipe!

T: I rush to the door to see if there are any other people out there?

GM: You don’t see or hear anything.

Escape

T: OK, I go back and untie the boy. He’s unconscious, so I’ll have to carry him. How much does he weigh?

GM: He’s around 60 lbs. That will make you Heavy Encumbrance (Encumbrance table B76) which for you is a move of 3. That’s running at Move 3 (3 yards/sec = about 6 mph). It will cost Fatigue (B130), if you run.

T: Can I fireman carry him over my left shoulder and keep my right hand free for my gun? Also, I replace the round I just shot at Alfred.

GM: Yes, you can do that. While you reload the gun… (rolls some dice) Alfred dies by drowning in his blood.

T: I didn’t see any other way out of this place on the way in. No windows?

GM: No, you just saw the door from the street into a hallway. That hallway had a door on the left as you entered and then the staircase going up to the frosted panel door. The hallway from the door at the top of the stair to the card playing room had no doors or windows. The card playing room has no windows and only the door to the stairs and the storeroom.

T: OK, I walk carefully with my gun ready and try to leave the building.

GM: (rolls some dice) You make it to 59th Street with no problem. The four thugs are still sleeping and no one is around at 11 PM down on the East River.

T: I make my way toward a normal street to find a cab. I’m still in disguise, right?

GM: Yes, you are.

… And that wraps our scene…

What This Example Demonstrates

1. Investigation Before Violence

Most of the mission is:

Shadowing
Stealth
Telepathy
Positioning

2. Surprise Is Power

+6 grapple bonus decided the fight.

3. Telepathy Is Surgical

Information extraction
Puppet control
Stun
Sleep
Target selection

4. Violence Is Brutal

A single .38 Special shot:
4 rolled damage
Became 8 at neck
Caused lethal collapse

This is not cinematic invulnerability.

5. Fatigue Matters

Mental Blow cost Fatigue.
Long missions require resource tracking.

6. Encumbrance Changes Escape

Carrying the boy reduces mobility.
GURPS models this.

Tone Note for Packet

Hyper-competence
Severe moral line (Pacifism: Cannot Harm Innocents)
Strategic brutality against predators
Telepathy as tactical advantage
Clean rules adjudication

It also demonstrates why the build runs:

-160 points in disadvantages
Extreme competence
Extreme pressure
