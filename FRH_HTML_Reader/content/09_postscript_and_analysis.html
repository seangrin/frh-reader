[2026-04-16T11:56:29.065Z] ASSISTANT (final_answer)

If I have to name one historical published TTRPG, the closest match to FRH in live play is **_Chill_**.

Not because it matches your cosmology best, and not because it matches your mechanics best, but because in actual table function it comes closest to what FRH *is doing*:
- a loose team of gifted or unusually capable investigators
- tied to a larger occult-support structure
- sent into episodic supernatural cases
- with room for monster-of-the-week, hidden conspiracies, travel, lore reveals, and recurring enemies
- while still letting the characters, not the metaplot, carry the campaign

The strongest modern public-facing analogue is probably **_Candela Obscura_**, and the strongest tonal/mechanical cousins are **_Pulp Cthulhu_**, **_Delta Green_**, **_Vaesen_**, and **_Unknown Armies_**. But if you force me to choose the one older game that FRH most resembles in live play, I land on **_Chill_**.

**Why Chill wins**
Historically, *Chill* cast the PCs as **envoys of S.A.V.E.**, a secret organization investigating and combating evil in the world([boardgamemanuals.fandom.com](https://boardgamemanuals.fandom.com/wiki/Chill_%28role-playing_game%29?utm_source=openai)). Structurally, that is very close to:
- Edgar and the Vault
- mission dispatch
- recurring occult travel
- flexible team composition
- adult-schedule episodic play
- “important protagonists” rather than disposable victims

FRH is much smarter, broader, stranger, and more metaphysically ambitious than *Chill*, but the **campaign skeleton** is very similar.

**Why it isn’t just Call of Cthulhu**
*Call of Cthulhu* and especially **_Pulp Cthulhu_** are very close on period texture, globe-trotting weird science, and investigative horror-adventure([chaosium.com](https://www.chaosium.com/pulp-cthulhu-hardcover?utm_source=openai))([chaosium.com](https://www.chaosium.com/blogout-now-the-cthulhu-by-gaslight-investigators-guide/?utm_source=openai)). But CoC’s default assumption is still “ordinary investigators in over their heads,” while FRH is much more:
- chosen protagonists
- asymmetrical power-fantasy ensemble
- clue vectors keyed to sheets
- cosmology as a playable superstructure rather than mainly a doom-background

So FRH often *looks* CoC-adjacent, but **plays more like a powered occult ensemble game**.

**Why it isn’t just Delta Green**
*Delta Green* is a superb modern comparison point because it’s about agents confronting conspiratorial unnatural horror at terrible personal cost([delta-green.com](https://www.delta-green.com/about/?utm_source=openai)). But Delta Green is much tighter, grimmer, and more institutional. FRH is looser, warmer, funnier, more pulp, and far more willing to let the party’s bizarre composition reshape genre. Delta Green is “the agency against the apocalypse.” FRH is “the chosen weirdos, the Vault, and whatever the cosmos does next.”

**Why it isn’t just Candela Obscura**
From the current scene, *Candela Obscura* is the cleanest surface comparison: a turn-of-the-century investigative occult game where varied specialists are brought together under an esoteric order to confront strange incursions([darringtonpress.com](https://darringtonpress.com/announcing-our-new-ttrpg-candela-obscura/?utm_source=openai))([darringtonpress.com](https://darringtonpress.com/candela/?utm_source=openai)). That is very close structurally. But FRH is broader in tone and source DNA:
- more pulp adventure
- more GURPS hardness under the hood
- more powers with differentiated metaphysics
- more humor and genre slippage
- more supers/WoD/In Nomine/Amber in the blender

So if someone asked for “the 2020s game that resembles FRH fastest,” I’d say **Candela Obscura**. If they asked for the **older published game that feels most like FRH in use**, I’d still say **Chill**.

**Why it isn’t just Unknown Armies**
This is the strongest **philosophical cousin**. *Unknown Armies* is explicitly an occult game about broken people, conspiracies, power, consequences, and reality shaped by belief([atlas-games.com](https://www.atlas-games.com/unknownarmies?utm_source=openai))([atlas-games.com](https://www.atlas-games.com/product_tables/AG6031?utm_source=openai)). That is very close to your deeper subtext. It also fits your Wilson/Illuminatus side better than Chill does. But UA is more urban-occult, more psychologically jagged, and less mission-frame/pulp-team oriented than FRH. It shares your **brain**, not your **campaign chassis**.

**Why it isn’t just Vaesen**
*Vaesen* is another strong current cousin: investigators, headquarters, period folklore horror, mysteries, and a clear campaign frame([freeleaguepublishing.com](https://freeleaguepublishing.com/games/vaesen/)). But it is narrower and more folkloric. FRH is bigger, messier, and more cosmologically hybrid.

**What the deeper ancestry looks like**
If I go all the way back to the hobby’s roots, I’d split the answer in two:

1. **Deepest play ancestor:** **Braunstein / Blackmoor**
- Braunstein (1969) is where players got roles, competing agendas, and the freedom to act beyond a fixed war-game script([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Braunstein_%28game%29?utm_source=openai)).
- Blackmoor turned that into ongoing campaign play with persistent characters([en.wikipedia.org](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackmoor_%28campaign_setting%29?utm_source=openai)).

That matters because your Session 8 philosophy statement about roleplaying versus storytelling is **Braunstein-descended** in spirit:
- lay out a charged situation
- let players act
- don’t predetermine the meaningful outcomes

2. **Closest historical published game:** **Chill**
- because it takes that freedom and puts it into an occult-investigator campaign frame closest to FRH’s live structure.

So my clean final answer is:

**FRH’s deepest ancestor is Braunstein-through-Blackmoor, but the single historical published TTRPG it most closely resembles in live play is _Chill_.**

If you want the short comparative formula, I’d put it like this:

**FRH = _Chill_’s campaign chassis + _Pulp Cthulhu_’s adventure energy + _Unknown Armies_’ belief-and-conspiracy brain + your GURPS/Amber/In Nomine/WoD synthesis.**
