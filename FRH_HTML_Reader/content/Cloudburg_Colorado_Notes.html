Cloudburg, Colorado Notes

(Fictional Town) Lat 37.11, Long -107.393

http://www.scribblemaps.com/create#id=VGWyx3jWce&lat=37.10462233&lng=-107.41004282&z=14&t=hybrid



Cloudburg is located in southern Colorado along what will be Hwy 151 about 8 miles SE of Chimney Rock National Monument or a 30 minute horse ride. The town was originally a settlement started by the Scotsman Connor MacLeod as he started his quest for gold in 1861.


Connor MacLeod immigrated from the Lochaber area of the Highlands of Scotland in 1850 and worked as a longshoreman in New York City saving money. In 1852, he traveled to Arkham, MA to study at Miskatonic University. Graduating in 1859 with a Masters of Science in Metallurgy and Bachelor of Science in Geology, Connor set out to find his fortune out West.


He established his settlement on a medium hill near the small lake, Lago Brillente, located along the Piedra River. In the center of the lake is a small island hosting a Greek Golden apple tree, a species not seen outside of the northeast regions of the Mediterranean. The Spanish explorers called the island Isla Oro. No one knows how the apple tree got to Colorado but it was here before the Spanish as the Ute people call it Panak(a) Pii'ay Tuwap (Golden Fruit Tree).


After Connor found gold and became filthy rich, the settlement grew to a town and became Cloudburg (A blend of misspelling part of his last name (Cloud) and his wife's German ancestry (burg - "castle on a hill")). When Colorado became a state in 1876, Cloudburg became the seat of Isla Oro County.


By 1880, the town had grown to 1,200 people and Connor established a university inviting professors from Miskatonic to join. In addition, a few scholars from outside the US joined the faculty. Isla Oro University opened for 13 students on August 26th, 1881. The school focuses on Geology, Metallurgy, and Anthropology.


In 1891, Connor's wife, Angela (nee Metz, German origin, Duchy of Lorraine), passed away. Connor created a trust for the University and township, then disappeared. The income from the MacLeod mine still funds the trust.


In 1923, Isla Oro University has 17 full-time professors and ~200 students. The town population is ~1,700 with the main business being mining,banking, and transportation. With the establishment of MacLeod Bank & Trust, a number of mining and transportation companies use their services. Since the town is very remote, the residents are mainly miners, cowboys, and service workers. The 200 students make up a large part of the culture. As a result, Cloudburg has good food and luxuries like good coffee and tea. The elite in town are predominately white from New England with ancestry from the British Isles, Germany,and France. The miners and service workers (cowboys, teamsters) are split between Hispanic and White with a few blacks. Among the population are some pure and mixed Ute Indians.
